Summary in one sentence: CallMask runs entirely on your device. It does not hold the internet permission, it sends nothing anywhere, it has no servers, no analytics and no ads — and uninstalling the app deletes all of its data.

1. Who is responsible

CallMask is developed and maintained independently by Juzo Luis Hatao Marinelli, an individual developer. For any question, request or complaint about privacy and data protection, contact juzoluis@gmail.com.

2. What the app does

CallMask blocks incoming phone calls based on number masks that you define (for example +5511*, 0800??????? or *9845*). While the app holds the Android call screening role (Android 10 or newer), the operating system hands it every incoming call before the phone rings, and the app tells the system whether the call should be rejected or allowed through.

3. What data is accessed

Data Source Purpose
Phone number of the incoming call Handed to the screening service by the Android system itself, at call time Matched against your rules (masks) to decide whether to block or allow the call
Device call log (READ_CALL_LOG permission) Your phone's own call log, read only when you open the “Calls” tab Show you the list of received/placed calls so you can select numbers and build blocking or allow masks from them
Your rules and blocked-call history Created by you inside the app Enforce blocking and show which calls were blocked, when, and by which mask

READ_CALL_LOG is the only permission declared by the application, besides the call screening role you explicitly grant in Android settings. The app does not request access to contacts, messages, microphone, camera, location, files, advertising identifiers or any other data source.

4. Where the data is stored

Everything stays on your device only, in a local database (Room/SQLite, file callblocker.db) inside the app's private storage, plus a simple preference for the “block hidden numbers” option. Specifically, the app stores:

  • Rules: the mask, a label you choose, the type (block or always allow), whether it is enabled, and the creation date.
  • Blocked-call history: the blocked number (or a “hidden” marker when the call has no caller ID), the mask that matched, and the date/time.

The device call log is not copied into the app's database: it is read on demand, only for display, while you are using the “Calls” tab. Allowed calls are not recorded by the app.

5. Nothing is transmitted

CallMask does not declare the internet permission (android.permission.INTERNET) in its manifest. This means the app is technically unable to open any network connection — this is not a promise, it is a restriction enforced by the operating system, and anyone can verify it by inspecting the manifest of the published APK itself (for example with aapt dump permissions or any APK inspection app) — you don't have to take my word for it. As a result:

  • there is no CallMask server, backend or “cloud”;
  • there is no analytics, telemetry, crash reporting or tracking;
  • there are no ads and no advertising SDKs;
  • there are no third-party data-collecting SDKs — the app only uses Android's own libraries (AndroidX, Jetpack Compose and Room), which work locally;
  • the developer has no access, and cannot gain access, to your numbers, rules or history.

An honest note about Android backup. The app allows Android's standard automatic backup. If you keep Android backup enabled in your device settings, the app's data may be included in your backup, in your Google account, just like other apps. That backup is performed and encrypted by the operating system, is controlled by you under Settings › Google › Backup, and neither CallMask nor the developer can access its content. You may disable backup for this app in Android settings if you prefer.

6. Data sharing and selling

There is no sharing of data with third parties, because there is no transmission of data at all. Your data is never sold, rented, licensed or used for advertising, profiling, credit scoring or any secondary purpose.

7. Retention and deletion

You are in full and immediate control, inside the app itself:

  • Delete a rule: remove it in the “Rules” tab.
  • Clear the history: use the option to clear the blocked-call history.
  • Delete everything at once: use Settings › Apps › CallMask › Storage › Clear data, or simply uninstall the app — Android removes the database and preferences along with it.
  • Revoke access: you can withdraw the call log permission and the screening role at any time in Android settings. Without the screening role, the app no longer receives calls to evaluate.

Since no data ever leaves the device, there is no copy held by the developer that could be requested, corrected or deleted.

8. Children

CallMask is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from minors. Since the app does not collect or transmit anyone's data, no child's information is under the developer's control. There is no login, account, profile or user-generated content.

9. Legal basis and your rights

All processing described here happens exclusively on your device, under your control, based on permissions you expressly grant in Android. To the extent that Brazil's General Data Protection Law (LGPD, Law No. 13,709/2018) applies — and, by analogy, comparable regimes such as the GDPR:

  • processing relies on the data subject's consent (LGPD art. 7, I), expressed by granting the call log permission and the screening role, and is limited to the specific purpose of deciding whether an incoming call should be blocked;
  • data minimisation is observed: only the incoming number and the call log are used, and only on the device;
  • the rights of access, correction, deletion, portability and withdrawal of consent (LGPD art. 18) are exercised directly in the app and in Android settings, as described in section 7 — the data is physically with you, not with the developer;
  • there is no international data transfer, because there is no transfer at all.

Data protection questions can be sent to juzoluis@gmail.com.

10. In-app purchases

Today CallMask has no in-app purchases, subscriptions or paid tier. If a one-time purchase (premium version) is offered in the future, it will be processed entirely by Google Play Billing: payment data is collected and processed by Google under the Google Privacy Policy, and the developer never receives or stores card numbers, bank details or billing addresses. The app would only check locally, against the Google Play service installed on the device, whether the purchase was made. This policy will be updated before any such change takes effect.

11. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “last updated” date at the top will change and the new version will take effect on this same page. Material changes — especially anything involving new permissions or any form of data transmission — will be clearly described in the app's release notes on Google Play.
